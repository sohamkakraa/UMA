User Flow

1
Sign up / Sign in
User enters email or phone. UMA sends OTP. After verification, account is created and a blank PatientStore is initialized in localStorage and optionally synced server-side.
OTP email / SMS localStorage init
2
Set up Profile
Name, date of birth, sex, allergies, conditions, primary care provider, next visit date. For women, optional period tracker with cycle length and last period date. Family members can be added here too.
Profile page Family profiles Cycle tracker (♀)
3
Upload a Medical PDF
User taps "Upload a file" on the Dashboard. Picks document type (Lab report, Prescription, Imaging, Bill, Other). UMA sends the PDF through a two-stage pipeline: LlamaParse extracts raw text (3 credits/page, 10 k free credits/month) then Claude Haiku structures it — far cheaper than full PDF vision. Falls back to Claude Sonnet full PDF when LlamaParse credits run out or key is absent. Extracts labs, medications, allergies, conditions, and a full markdown record. Preview shows extractor badge (LlamaParse or Claude full PDF), tokens used, and total USD cost.
⚡ LlamaParse + Claude Haiku (primary) fallback: Claude Sonnet PDF uma-meta JSON + markdown Cost + extractor shown in UI Duplicate detection Patient name check
4
Review & Save to Records
UMA shows the extracted title, summary, badges (type, date, provider), and cost. If there's a patient name mismatch, user is warned and can confirm. On save, labs merge into trend charts, meds merge into the active medication list, allergies/conditions union into profile.
smartMergeExtractedDoc Lab deduplication Med deduplication
5
Explore the Dashboard
Health trends chart shows biomarkers (HbA1c, LDL, Glucose, etc.) over time. Medication tracker shows active meds with dose, frequency, and intake logs. Recent reports timeline. Blood pressure log. Quick profile snapshot with cycle badge (♀). Print-ready visit summary for clinician appointments.
Recharts area charts Medication tracker Health logs Cycle badge (♀)
6
Chat with Uma
User opens Chat. Uma has full PatientStore as context. User can ask "What was my last HbA1c?", "Explain my blood test", "Did I take my Metformin?". Attaching a PDF in chat runs the extraction pipeline in parallel — Uma replies while the document is being read. Cost is logged per message. Usage panel (📊 icon) shows total spend and per-message breakdown.
Claude Haiku (chat) Claude Sonnet (PDF in chat) Usage panel Family member context
7
Manage Family Members
Account holder adds a family member (mum, dad, spouse, child, etc.) from Profile. Each member has a completely isolated PatientStore (separate localStorage key). Switcher in top nav lets you jump between members. Dashboard and Chat reflect the active member with a banner and context.
Family switcher Isolated stores Context banners
8
Ongoing: Reminders, Logs, Exports
Log medication intake (taken / missed / skipped) from chat or Dashboard. Blood pressure log. Chat suggests reminders (UI renders set-reminder quick-reply chip). Export a print-ready visit summary PDF for your doctor.
Health logs BP tracker Visit summary export

Planned Features (Roadmap)

1
Cost-optimised PDF pipeline ✅ shipped
LlamaParse (10 k free credits/month, 3 credits/page) is now the primary PDF extractor. It handles OCR and layout; Claude Haiku then structures the pre-extracted text at text-token prices — typically 5–10x cheaper than sending the raw PDF to Claude Sonnet. The pipeline auto-falls back to Claude Sonnet full PDF mode when LlamaParse credits are exhausted (HTTP 402) or the key is absent. The upload preview shows which extractor ran, how many LlamaParse credits were consumed, and the total Anthropic cost.
2
Injection & recurring treatment tracker
Separate tracker for periodic injections (B12, insulin, vaccinations) with a countdown to the next due date shown on Dashboard. Integrates with the notification center for auto-reminders.
3
Appointment booking & scheduling
Integrate with hospital/clinic scheduling APIs. Surface available slots from within chat. Uma suggests specialists based on conditions + location. On booking confirmation, the patient's records are pre-shared with the clinic so the doctor can prepare before the visit.
4
Doctor's Dashboard (Clinician View)
A separate authenticated view for clinicians. A doctor can see all their registered patients in one place: health timelines, latest labs, medication lists, and symptom logs. When an appointment is booked, the patient's full record is automatically surfaced so the doctor can prepare their schedule. When a patient logs a notable symptom or a lab result goes out of range, the doctor receives a push notification and can prescribe follow-up tests or send a message directly from the dashboard — without waiting for the next visit.
5
Insurance management
Store insurance policy numbers, provider names, coverage limits, and copay details. Automatically flag which of the patient's existing documents (bills, prescriptions) are eligible for claim, and pre-fill claim forms from stored record data. Track reimbursement status and outstanding balances. For the doctor dashboard: surface the patient's insurance plan at appointment time so billing is handled upfront.
6
Patient email extraction (zero-upload)
Connect the patient's email inbox (Gmail / Outlook OAuth). The agent scans for lab result emails, hospital report attachments, prescription notifications, and appointment confirmations. New documents are automatically extracted and added to records — no manual upload required. Acts as a complement to FHIR connectors for providers that still send results by email.
7
Wearables & health sensor integration
Ingest continuous health signals from Apple Health, Google Fit, Fitbit, Garmin, Oura, Whoop, and CGM devices (Dexcom, Libre). Data flows into the same lab/vitals timeline as manual entries. Uma can correlate wearable trends (sleep, HRV, glucose spikes) against medication schedules and flag anomalies proactively. Doctor dashboard surfaces wearable trends alongside clinical records.
8
Hereditary & family health risk intelligence
The agent analyses health conditions across the family profiles stored in an account (mother, father, siblings, grandparents). When a heritable condition is identified in one member (e.g. Type 2 diabetes, hypertension, BRCA gene markers), UMA flags the at-risk family members and the carrier with plain-language explanations and recommended precautions — screening schedules, lifestyle adjustments, or specialist referrals. Both parties receive a notification so they can act. Never diagnostic; always directs to a clinician for confirmation.
9
Hospital database connectors (FHIR)
FHIR R4-compliant integrations to pull records directly from visited hospitals and clinics. Replaces manual PDF upload for supported providers. Combines with email extraction and wearables to give UMA a near-complete picture of the patient's health without any manual effort.
10
Backend persistence & multi-device sync
Move from localStorage to a proper encrypted database. PatientStore synced across all devices in real time. End-to-end encryption with patient-controlled keys. Audit log for every access event. Required before enabling the Doctor Dashboard (clinician data access must be logged and revocable).
11
Wellness check-in loop
Gentle daily/weekly prompts in chat asking how the user feels (mood, symptoms, energy). One question at a time, never a form. Symptom notes stored over time for trend analysis and surfaced to the doctor dashboard when relevant (e.g. consistent fatigue logged before a scheduled visit).
